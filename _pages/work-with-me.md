I work with tissue imaging groups in two ways. Read both and pick the one that fits your project. If you are not sure, describe the project and I will tell you which track makes sense. Overall I am interested in collaborating with domain experts with hands on/hands off experience in exploring complex data structures.

<div class="work-tracks">

<div class="work-track">
  <h2>Consulting</h2>
  <p>For teams that need a specific analysis done well and done soon. I scope the work, deliver publication ready results and code, and you keep full ownership. No authorship expected.</p>
  <p><strong>Good fit when:</strong></p>
  <ul>
    <li>You have imaging or spatial data and a defined analysis question.</li>
    <li>You need spatial statistics that go beyond standard pipelines.</li>
    <li>You want reproducible code and figures you can drop into a paper or report.</li>
  </ul>
  <p><strong>How it works:</strong> short scoping call, fixed price for a defined scope, agreed timeline.</p>
  <p><a href="mailto:bhadury@umich.edu?subject=Consulting%20inquiry" class="btn btn--primary">Start a consulting project</a></p>
</div>

<div class="work-track">
  <h2>Research collaboration</h2>
  <p>For projects with real methods novelty and a paper at the end. Here I contribute methods development and analysis as a co-author rather than for a fee, and we share data and credit.</p>
  <p><strong>Good fit when:</strong></p>
  <ul>
    <li>Your question needs a method that does not exist yet, or a real extension of one of mine.</li>
    <li>You are aiming for a strong journal and want a methods co-author on the team.</li>
    <li>You can share the data and are set up for a genuine collaboration.</li>
  </ul>
  <p><strong>How it works:</strong> we talk through the question, agree on scope and authorship up front, and work it as a joint project.</p>
  <p><a href="mailto:bhadury@umich.edu?subject=Collaboration%20proposal" class="btn btn--primary">Propose a collaboration</a></p>
</div>

</div>

## What I do not take on

To keep quality high I take a small number of projects at a time. Routine analysis that a general bioinformatics core can handle is usually better placed there. I focus on the spatial and Bayesian modeling that is hard to get elsewhere.
